<p>The <b>Talent League</b> (also known as the <b><a href="/wiki/Coates_Hire" title="Coates Hire">Coates</a> Talent League</b> under <a href="/wiki/Naming_rights" title="Naming rights">naming rights</a><sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and previously as the <b><a href="/wiki/National_Australia_Bank" title="National Australia Bank">NAB</a> League </b>and <b><a href="/wiki/Transport_Accident_Commission" title="Transport Accident Commission">TAC</a> Cup</b>) is an under-19 <a href="/wiki/Australian_rules_football" title="Australian rules football">Australian rules football</a> representative competition based in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a> and run by the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Football_League" title="Australian Football League">Australian Football League</a> (AFL). It is based on geographic regions throughout country <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" class="mw-redirect" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victoria</a> and metropolitan <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a> with each team representing one of twelve Victorian regions, while a thirteenth team from <a href="/wiki/Tasmania" title="Tasmania">Tasmania</a> was reintroduced in 2019.
</p><p>The competition is the primary sources of recruitment for the clubs of the AFL with around three quarters of all players selected from its ranks in each <a href="/wiki/AFL_draft" class="mw-redirect" title="AFL draft">AFL draft</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It provides an opportunity for talented young regional players to participate in a high standard competition without having to relocate too far from their place of origin. The competition has a very successful pathway with players missing AFL selection often being recruited by semi-professional state, country and regional leagues throughout Australia. An equivalent competition for female footballers, known as the <a href="/wiki/Talent_League_Girls" title="Talent League Girls">Talent League Girls</a>, is also contested on an annual basis.
</p><p>The league was known as the TAC Cup until 2018, the NAB League in 2019 to 2022, and since 2023 it has been known as the Coates Talent League.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Talent_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>With the focus of the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Football_League" title="Australian Football League">VFL/AFL</a> moving rapidly toward a national competition, the former metropolitan and country <a href="/wiki/Zoning_(Australian_rules_football)" title="Zoning (Australian rules football)">zoning</a> recruitment system for the Victorian VFL/AFL clubs was abolished, and the league's under-19 competition was shut down at the end of 1991.
</p><p>A new competition, administered by the <a href="/wiki/Victorian_State_Football_League" title="Victorian State Football League">Victorian State Football League</a> and sponsored by the <a href="/wiki/Transport_Accident_Commission" title="Transport Accident Commission">Transport Accident Commission</a>, was formed as an avenue for young Victorian under-18 players to make the transition to becoming senior <a href="/wiki/Australian_Football_League" title="Australian Football League">League</a> players. It commenced play on 10 April 1992, the competition consisted of five metropolitan teams and one country team: the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Knights_Football_Club" title="Northern Knights Football Club">Northern Knights</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Ranges" title="Eastern Ranges">Eastern Ranges</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dandenong_Stingrays" title="Dandenong Stingrays">Southern Stingrays (renamed the Dandenong Stingrays in 1995)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Western_Jets" title="Western Jets">Western Jets</a>, <a href="/wiki/Central_Dragons" class="mw-redirect" title="Central Dragons">Central Dragons (renamed the Prahran Dragons in 1995, then the Sandringham Dragons in 2000)</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Geelong_Falcons" title="Geelong Falcons">Geelong Falcons</a>.
</p><p>In 1993 an additional four country teams were included – the <a href="/wiki/Murray_Bushrangers" title="Murray Bushrangers">Murray Bushrangers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bendigo_Pioneers" title="Bendigo Pioneers">Bendigo Pioneers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gippsland_Power" title="Gippsland Power">Gippsland Power</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greater_Western_Victoria_Rebels" title="Greater Western Victoria Rebels">Ballarat Rebels (renamed the North Ballarat Rebels in 1996, then the Greater Western Victoria Rebels in 2017)</a>. In 1995 two additional metropolitan regions were established, with the <a href="/wiki/Oakleigh_Chargers" title="Oakleigh Chargers">Oakleigh Chargers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Calder_Cannons" title="Calder Cannons">Calder Cannons</a> teams included in the competition.
</p><p>In 1995, a Tasmanian-based team, the <a href="/wiki/Tassie_Mariners" title="Tassie Mariners">Tassie Mariners</a>, commenced in the competition, becoming the league's first non-Victorian side. The following year, the <a href="/wiki/NSW/ACT_Rams" title="NSW/ACT Rams">NSW/ACT Rams</a> were admitted. The Mariners and Rams both exited the competition as full-time members at the end of the 2002, returning the competition to twelve teams.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Gold_Coast_Football_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="Gold Coast Football Club">Gold Coast Football Club</a> recruited several under-18s players in the 2008/09 summer, and participated in the TAC Cup in 2009 (before playing in the <a href="/wiki/Victorian_Football_League" title="Victorian Football League">VFL</a> in 2010 and the AFL from 2011). Similarly, the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Western_Sydney_Giants" title="Greater Western Sydney Giants">Greater Western Sydney Giants</a> fielded a TAC Cup team in 2010, two seasons prior to its introduction to the AFL in 2012.
</p><p>Four interstate teams — the Tassie Mariners, NSW/ACT Rams, Queensland Scorpions and the Northern Territory Thunder — each play a handful of games each year against TAC Cup teams, particularly in the lead-up to the annual <a href="/wiki/AFL_Under_18_Championships" class="mw-redirect" title="AFL Under 18 Championships">AFL Under 18 Championships</a>; these games are counted as part of the TAC Cup premiership season, but the interstate clubs are not eligible for the premiership.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Between 1995 and 2008, the finals system was in a knock-out format.  This reverted to a traditional finals system in 2009 with the introduction of the Gold Coast team.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2010, this was extended to include 12 of the 13 clubs participating that season, with the extra matches forming an extended knockout format. In 2011 the finals system was reverted to the traditional eight-team AFL finals series. Prior to the 2014 season, the <a href="/wiki/NSW/ACT_Rams" title="NSW/ACT Rams">NSW/ACT Rams</a> was reestablished as a TAC Cup team, with players from the <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Swans" title="Sydney Swans">Sydney Swans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greater_Western_Sydney_Giants" title="Greater Western Sydney Giants">Greater Western Sydney Giants</a> young academy sides being picked for the NSW/ACT team.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>From 2019, the newly named NAB League introduced six new teams: the AFL Academy sides of <a href="/wiki/Gold_Coast_Suns" title="Gold Coast Suns">Gold Coast</a>, <a href="/wiki/GWS_Giants" class="mw-redirect" title="GWS Giants">GWS Giants</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Swans" title="Sydney Swans">Sydney Swans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brisbane_Lions" title="Brisbane Lions">Brisbane Lions</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Territory" title="Northern Territory">Northern Territory</a>; and the returning Tassie Mariners, who were later renamed the <a href="/wiki/Tasmania_Devils_(NAB_League)" class="mw-redirect" title="Tasmania Devils (NAB League)">Tasmania Devils</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Additionally, teams were able to include more 19-year-olds – previously only three could be selected.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The 2020 season was cancelled due to the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>, and in 2021 the competition fully transitioned from under-18s to under-19s – although the draft age to senior football remained at 18.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Since 2023 the league has been primarily by under-18 players, with a selection of under-19 players also listed.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Age_eligibility">Age eligibility</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Talent_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Age eligibility"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>For many years the league was primarily a competition for 18-year-olds, though exceptions were made for bottom-aged players—16- or 17-year-olds—and since 2007, over-age players—19-year-olds—to participate in the competition. In 2021 the league shifted to an under-19 level, though the entry age for the <a href="/wiki/AFL_Draft" class="mw-redirect" title="AFL Draft">AFL Draft</a> remains 18.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Since the beginning of the 2007 TAC Cup season, clubs have been granted permission to select up to five over-age players permitted on their lists.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Nonetheless, age eligibility requirements remain for the <a href="/wiki/AFL_Draft" class="mw-redirect" title="AFL Draft">AFL Draft</a>, where players must have turned seventeen years of age by 30 April of that draft year to be eligible for selection by an AFL club.
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Awards">Awards</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Talent_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Morrish_Medal">Morrish Medal</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Talent_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Morrish Medal"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Morrish_Medal" title="Morrish Medal">Morrish Medal</a> is awarded to the best player in the competition each year. The same medal was previously awarded to the best player in the <a href="/wiki/Victorian_Football_League_(1897%E2%80%931989)" class="mw-redirect" title="Victorian Football League (1897–1989)">Victorian Football League</a> Thirds/Under-19s competition, which the TAC Cup superseded.
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="TAC_Cup_Coaches_Award">TAC Cup Coaches Award</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Talent_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: TAC Cup Coaches Award"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>The TAC Cup Coaches Award is voted on by both coaches in a 5–4–3–2–1 format at the end of each game.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> At the end of the 2015 season, the award was discontinued.
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Coverage">Coverage</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Talent_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Coverage"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>

</p><p>Former coverage included:
</p>
<ul><li>The Match of the Round being broadcast live on Rumble 103, an internet radio station based in Melbourne.</li>
<li>A TAC Cup show screening on <a href="/wiki/Nine_Network" title="Nine Network">Channel 9</a> in Melbourne and across Victoria and Inland NSW border regions on <a href="/wiki/WIN_Television" title="WIN Television">WIN TV</a> from 1 p.m. every Sunday from late March until the end of September.</li>
<li>TAC Cup news and results being seen on <a href="/wiki/C31_Melbourne" title="C31 Melbourne">C31</a>'s Local Footy Show and heard on the ABC's <a href="/wiki/Triple_J" title="Triple J">Triple J</a> radio station.</li>

<li>Radio stations SYN FM &amp; RRR FM broadcast coverage of TAC Cup football matches every weekend during the season.</li></ul>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="VFL_affiliations">VFL affiliations</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Talent_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: VFL affiliations"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Nine of the 12 Victorian-based Talent League clubs are affiliated with a <a href="/wiki/Victorian_Football_League" title="Victorian Football League">Victorian Football League</a> (VFL) club. This allows for a development pathway between under-18s football and state-level senior football, with top-age players permitted to play senior VFL games under the 23rd man rule:
</p>
<ul><li>Calder Cannons - <a href="/wiki/Coburg_Football_Club" title="Coburg Football Club">Coburg</a></li>
<li>Dandenong Stingrays - <a href="/wiki/Frankston_Football_Club" title="Frankston Football Club">Frankston</a></li>
<li>Eastern Ranges - <a href="/wiki/Box_Hill_Hawks" title="Box Hill Hawks">Box Hill</a></li>
<li>Geelong Falcons - <a href="/wiki/Werribee_Football_Club" title="Werribee Football Club">Werribee</a></li>
<li>Gippsland Power - <a href="/wiki/Casey_Demons" title="Casey Demons">Casey Demons</a></li>
<li>Northern Knights - <a href="/wiki/Northern_Bullants" title="Northern Bullants">Northern Bullants</a></li>
<li>Oakleigh Chargers - <a href="/wiki/Port_Melbourne_Football_Club" title="Port Melbourne Football Club">Port Melbourne</a></li>
<li>Sandringham Dragons - <a href="/wiki/Sandringham_Football_Club" title="Sandringham Football Club">Sandringham</a></li>
