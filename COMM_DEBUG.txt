(/ˈʃæki/ [/wiki/Help:IPA/English] SHAK-ee\\\\n[/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key];[1] born 21 August 1997) is an\\\\nAustralian rules footballer [/wiki/Australian_rules_football] who Football League [/wiki/Australian_Football_League] (AFL).\\\\n\\\\n\\\\nEARLY LIFE\\\\n\\\\n[edit [/w/index.php?title=Josh_Schache&action=edit&section=1]]\\\\n\\\\nSchache was born in Adelaide, South Australia [/wiki/Adelaide,_South_Australia]\\\\nto mother Rachel and father Laurence [/wiki/Laurence_Schache].[2] He started\\\\nplaying Auskick [/wiki/Auskick] when he was in grade five. He attended high\\\\nschool at Assumption College Kilmore [/wiki/Assumption_College,_Kilmore] and\\\\nlater Goulburn Valley Grammar School [/wiki/Goulburn_Valley_Grammar_School]. His\\\\nlate father, Laurence [/wiki/Laurence_Schache], also played with Brisbane in the\\\\nearly 1990s.[3][4][5]\\\\n\\\\nJosh spent his early youth as a ruckman/forward for the Seymour Lions, a club\\\\nbased in the Seymour District Junior Football League. He played with the Murray\\\\nBushrangers [/wiki/Murray_Bushrangers] in the TAC Cup [/wiki/TAC_Cup] until he\\\\nwas selected to join the Brisbane Lions with the second pick in the 2015 AFL\\\\ndraft [/wiki/2015_AFL_draft].[4]\\\\n\\\\nSchache is left-footed, 199 centimetres (78 in) tall and weighs 96 kilograms\\\\n(212 lb).[6]\\\\n\\\\nHe won the Larke Medal [/wiki/Larke_Medal] for his performance in the NAB AFL\\\\nUnder-18 Championships in 2015, where he also he kicked a record 24 goals.[6]\\\\n\\\\n\\\\nAFL CAREER\\\\n\\\\n[edit [/w/index.php?title=Josh_Schache&action=edit&section=2]]\\\\n\\\\nIn the first two years of his career at the Brisbane Lions\\\\n[/wiki/Brisbane_Lions], Schache played 27 games and kicked 25 goals. During 2017\\\\nthere was speculation that he would request a trade to a Victorian club, but at\\\\nthe end of the season his manager shut down speculation, insisting that he would\\\\nnot be requesting a trade. Despite this, Brisbane later announced they would be\\\\nexploring trade options for Schache in the forthcoming trade period. In the\\\\nfinal minutes of the trade period, Schache was traded to the Western Bulldogs\\\\nfor pick 25 and 40 despite being contracted until 2019 at the Brisbane\\\\nLions.[7]Schache overcame niggling injuries during the first half of 2018. After\\\\nstrong performances in the VFL he was elevated to the senior side where he\\\\nfinished the season with 17 goals in 13 games.[8]Schache had a breakout year in\\\\n2019, kicking 24 goals from 14 games, only being omitted from the side due to\\\\nconcussion protocols. Schache played in the 2019 Elimination Final against club\\\\nrivals, Greater Western Sydney. After playing his career best year, Schache only\\\\nmanaged to play two senior games in 2020, kicking 2 goals.\\\\n\\\\nAfter a one-off appearance earlier in 2021, he returned to the senior side in\\\\nthe middle of the season, successfully moving to become a key defender.[9]\\\\nSchache managed to play every game for the rest of the year, as a defender too,\\\\nincluding the clubs impressive climb to the Grand Final. Schache was moved back\\\\nto the forward line for the 2021 finals [/wiki/2021_AFL_finals_series], kicking\\\\n4 goals and shutting down prominent key defenders such as Aliir Aliir\\\\n[/wiki/Aliir_Aliir] of Port Adelaide [/wiki/Port_Adelaide_Football_Club].[10] He\\\\nplayed in the Western Bulldogs' 2021 Grand Final [/wiki/2021_AFL_Grand_Final]\\\\nteam.[11]\\\\n\\\\nFollowing the 2022 AFL season [/wiki/2022_AFL_season], Schache was traded to\\\\nMelbourne [/wiki/Melbourne_Football_Club] on the last day of the trade\\\\nperiod.[12] Schache managed only four games across two seasons for the Demons.\\\\nWhen Melbourne failed to make finals for the first time since 2020, Schache was\\\\ndelisted at the conclusion of the 2024 AFL season [/wiki/2024_AFL_season].[13]\\\\n\\\\nIn 2025, Schache will play for the Doncaster East Football Club\\\\n[/wiki/Doncaster_East_Football_Club] in the Eastern Football (/ˈʃæki/ [/wiki/Help:IPA/English] SHAK-ee\\\\n[/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key];[1] born 21 August 1997) is an\\\\nAustralian rules footballer [/wiki/Australian_rules_football] who Football League [/wiki/Australian_Football_League] (AFL).\\\\n\\\\n\\\\nEARLY LIFE\\\\n\\\\n[edit [/w/index.php?title=Josh_Schache&action=edit&section=1]]\\\\n\\\\nSchache was born in Adelaide, South Australia [/wiki/Adelaide,_South_Australia]\\\\nto mother Rachel and father Laurence [/wiki/Laurence_Schache].[2] He started\\\\nplaying Auskick [/wiki/Auskick] when he was in grade five. He attended high\\\\nschool at Assumption College Kilmore [/wiki/Assumption_College,_Kilmore] and\\\\nlater Goulburn Valley Grammar School [/wiki/Goulburn_Valley_Grammar_School]. His\\\\nlate father, Laurence [/wiki/Laurence_Schache], also played with Brisbane in the\\\\nearly 1990s.[3][4][5]\\\\n\\\\nJosh spent his early youth as a ruckman/forward for the Seymour Lions, a club\\\\nbased in the Seymour District Junior Football League. He played with the Murray\\\\nBushrangers [/wiki/Murray_Bushrangers] in the TAC Cup [/wiki/TAC_Cup] until he\\\\nwas selected to join the Brisbane Lions with the second pick in the 2015 AFL\\\\ndraft [/wiki/2015_AFL_draft].[4]\\\\n\\\\nSchache is left-footed, 199 centimetres (78 in) tall and weighs 96 kilograms\\\\n(212 lb).[6]\\\\n\\\\nHe won the Larke Medal [/wiki/Larke_Medal] for his performance in the NAB AFL\\\\nUnder-18 Championships in 2015, where he also he kicked a record 24 goals.[6]\\\\n\\\\n\\\\nAFL CAREER\\\\n\\\\n[edit [/w/index.php?title=Josh_Schache&action=edit&section=2]]\\\\n\\\\nIn the first two years of his career at the Brisbane Lions\\\\n[/wiki/Brisbane_Lions], Schache played 27 games and kicked 25 goals. During 2017\\\\nthere was speculation that he would request a trade to a Victorian club, but at\\\\nthe end of the season his manager shut down speculation, insisting that he would\\\\nnot be requesting a trade. Despite this, Brisbane later announced they would be\\\\nexploring trade options for Schache in the forthcoming trade period. In the\\\\nfinal minutes of the trade period, Schache was traded to the Western Bulldogs\\\\nfor pick 25 and 40 despite being contracted until 2019 at the Brisbane\\\\nLions.[7]Schache overcame niggling injuries during the first half of 2018. After\\\\nstrong performances in the VFL he was elevated to the senior side where he\\\\nfinished the season with 17 goals in 13 games.[8]Schache had a breakout year in\\\\n2019, kicking 24 goals from 14 games, only being omitted from the side due to\\\\nconcussion protocols. Schache played in the 2019 Elimination Final against club\\\\nrivals, Greater Western Sydney. After playing his career best year, Schache only\\\\nmanaged to play two senior games in 2020, kicking 2 goals.\\\\n\\\\nAfter a one-off appearance earlier in 2021, he returned to the senior side in\\\\nthe middle of the season, successfully moving to become a key defender.[9]\\\\nSchache managed to play every game for the rest of the year, as a defender too,\\\\nincluding the clubs impressive climb to the Grand Final. Schache was moved back\\\\nto the forward line for the 2021 finals [/wiki/2021_AFL_finals_series], kicking\\\\n4 goals and shutting down prominent key defenders such as Aliir Aliir\\\\n[/wiki/Aliir_Aliir] of Port Adelaide [/wiki/Port_Adelaide_Football_Club].[10] He\\\\nplayed in the Western Bulldogs' 2021 Grand Final [/wiki/2021_AFL_Grand_Final]\\\\nteam.[11]\\\\n\\\\nFollowing the 2022 AFL season [/wiki/2022_AFL_season], Schache was traded to\\\\nMelbourne [/wiki/Melbourne_Football_Club] on the last day of the trade\\\\nperiod.[12] Schache managed only four games across two seasons for the Demons.\\\\nWhen Melbourne failed to make finals for the first time since 2020, Schache was\\\\ndelisted at the conclusion of the 2024 AFL season [/wiki/2024_AFL_season].[13]\\\\n\\\\nIn 2025, Schache will play for the Doncaster East Football Club\\\\n[/wiki/Doncaster_East_Football_Club] in the Eastern Football Unlike\\\\nEuropean languages, with their Biblical and Greco-Roman heritage, the Chinese\\\\nlanguage [/wiki/Chinese_language] does not have a particular set of words\\\\nreserved for given names: any combination of Chinese characters can\\\\ntheoretically be used as a given name. For example, in England and Wales, the most popular female\\\\nand male names given to babies born in 1800 were Mary and John, with 24% of\\\\nfemale babies and 22% of male babies receiving those names, respectively.[32] In\\\\ncontrast, the corresponding statistics for England and Wales in 1994 were Emily\\\\nand James, with 3% and 4% of names, respectively. Not only have Mary and John\\\\ngone out of favour in the English-speaking world, but the overall distribution\\\\nof names has also changed significantly over the last 100 years for females, but\\\\nnot for males. This has led to an increasing amount of diversity for female\\\\nnames.[33]\\\\n\\\\n\\\\nCHOICE OF NAMES\\\\n\\\\n[edit [/w/index.php?title=Given_name&action=edit&section=9]]\\\\n\\\\nEducation, ethnicity, religion, class and political ideology affect parents'\\\\nchoice of names.
